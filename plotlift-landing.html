Why this needs to exist

Getting numbers out of a published chart is still a 6-step chore.

Every researcher has done it. The data you need is right there in the figure — but locked inside a picture. The tools that free it haven't kept up.

01

The free tool is clunky

Screenshot the figure, open a separate site, upload it, calibrate the axes by hand, click each point. Then do it again for the next figure.

02

Auto-detect grabs noise

Existing digitizers pick up gridlines, legend markers, and axis ticks instead of your actual data — and can't tell two overlapping curves apart.

03

AI just makes numbers up

Pasting a chart into a chatbot is fast, but it quietly invents values — and accuracy collapses on exactly the dual-axis, low-res figures you care about.

How Plotlift works

Right where the figure already is.

RIGHT-CLICK

Point at the chart

On any webpage or open PDF, right-click the figure and choose “Lift data.” The extension reads it in place — nothing to download or upload.

VERIFY

Check every point

Detected points are drawn back onto the original image. Drag, add, or delete any that look off. You confirm the data — the tool never hides its guesses.

EXPORT

Take the numbers

Copy straight to clipboard or export CSV, Excel, or JSON. Back to your analysis in seconds, with data you actually trust.
